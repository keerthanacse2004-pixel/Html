FREEDOM FIGHTERS OF INDIA

Mahatma Gandhi is best known for leading the Indian independence movement through nonviolent resistance, known as satyagraha. He advocated for truth and justice, using peaceful protests and civil disobedience to challenge British rule and achieve political and social progress
Bhagat Singh's views on love are most evident in his notebook, where he quotes Charles Dickens, calling love "the most interesting and pardonable of human weaknesses". He also viewed sex as a biological necessity, similar to hunger, and refused to judge it based on morality or legality
Rani Lakshmibai, also known as the Rani of Jhansi, is famous for her exceptional bravery and leadership during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. She is renowned for her fierce resistance against the British East India Company's expansionist policies and her prominent role in the fight for India's independence.
Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel is primarily known as the "Unifier of India" for his crucial role in integrating the 565 princely states into the newly independent nation after British rule ended. He also earned the moniker "Iron Man of India" for his strong leadership and decisive actions in consolidating the nation.
He was one of the first advocates of Swaraj or self-rule. He gave the slogan, “Swaraj is my birthright and I shall have it.” He believed that no progress was possible without self-rule. He was part of the extremist faction of the INC and was a proponent of boycott and Swadeshi movements.
Subhash Chandra Bose is famous for his prominent role in India's fight for independence from British rule. He is particularly known for forming the Indian National Army (INA) and leading it in a march towards India, with the aim of liberating the country.
